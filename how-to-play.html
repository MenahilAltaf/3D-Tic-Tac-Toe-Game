GUIDE

How to play TRIAD

Everything you need to go from first click to first win — objective, rules, controls, and what a winning board looks like.

01 · OBJECTIVE

Claim three tiles in a straight line.

TRIAD is played on a 3×3 board rendered in full 3D. Two players alternate placing pieces — an X or an O — until one of them lines up three matching pieces in a row, column, or diagonal.

02 · RULES

The rules, in full.

✕

X always moves first

In vs-AI mode you play as X and always take the opening move. In 2-player mode, X and O alternate starting each new round.

◯

One piece per turn

Click any open tile to place your piece. Occupied tiles can't be selected again for the rest of the round.

⌗

Eight possible winning lines

Three rows, three columns, and two diagonals — any of them completed with matching pieces ends the game instantly.

＝

A full board with no line is a draw

If all nine tiles fill up with no winning line, the round ends in a draw and adds to your draw count.

03 · CONTROLS

Move the camera like it's your own board.

05 · STEP-BY-STEP

Your first round, walked through.

01

Choose a mode

On the Play Game page, pick "vs AI" for a solo challenge or "2 Players" to share the board with a friend.

02

Get comfortable with the camera

Drag to orbit and scroll to zoom until you can see the whole board clearly.

03

Place your first piece

Hover over a tile to preview it, then click to lock in your move. The timer and move counter start immediately.

04

Watch the opponent respond

Against the AI, a short "thinking" pause plays before its move appears. In 2-player mode, pass control to the next player.

05

Close out the win

Complete a line to trigger the win animation and confetti, then check the Leaderboard page to see where your run ranks.
